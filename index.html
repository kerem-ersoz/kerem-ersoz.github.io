Kerem's Forks of Suckless Software

These are the forks of some of the tools that the suckless team have put out. I am a fan of their work; my forks slightly extend the spartan functionality of their tiling window manager dwm, their terminal emulator st, and the well-known dmenu by adding some GNU-specific functionality, as well as the suckless-provided patches I've installed and tweaks I've made to make it behave better on my computers. I tried to preserve their "sucklessness" as much as possible, i.e. they are still less bloated than many alternatives.

Kekken - Yet Another 2D Fighter Engine

Kekken is a badly-named 2D fighting game engine I made using SDL2 and its related utilities. I learned a good bit about flow control in games/interactive interfaces and concepts like collision detection using AABBs and ray tracing, rendering and blitting, and the quadtree data structure that allowed for efficient iteration through the screen when searching for collisions. It is extendable with many options for frame data, modeled after other well-known engines like M.A.M.E. and Mugen.

Molecular Solvation Energy Calculator

This utility was presented to me as an idea by the UMN faculty Yuk Sham. Based on the Ab Initio methods used by Nobel laureate A. Warshel and his research group, this program calculates the solvation energy of an organic molecule in a solution represented by "grid points". It can read output from the cheminformatics suite Desmond, and produce forcefield output that is compatible with it as well.

Microbiomes Visualized in R

A collection of scripts I wrote up that takes raw DNA sample data of bacteria from various places in the body, maps it over its metadata, and classifies it in terms of bacteria. Then, it runs Pairwise Wilcox Tests or ADONIS tests on clusters of data as appropriate, and ggplots them in either boxplots of abundance over samples or principal coordinate analysis plots of differences between samples.
